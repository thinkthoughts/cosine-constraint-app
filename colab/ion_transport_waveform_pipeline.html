A compact bridge from segmented-trap ion transport papers and job-description requirements into a reproducible simulation pipeline: electrode basis → moving potential well → voltage waveform → ion motion → excitation metric.

One-line translation

Smooth transport waveforms reduce motional excitation by making the moving trap center continuous enough that the ion follows rather than rings.

\[ m\ddot{x}(t) = -m\omega^2\big[x(t)-x_c(t)\big] \]

The page is intentionally simple: it shows a reproducible path from trajectory design to excitation scaling, rather than claiming a full hardware-specific optimizer.

Pipeline diagram

The project turns ion shuttling into a readable control pipeline. Each stage produces either a figure, a saved data object, or a reusable module.

1

Electrode basis

Construct simplified spatial basis functions for segmented electrodes.

2

Potential well

Solve electrode voltages that place an approximate harmonic well at target positions.

3

Transport path

Move the well center \(x_c(t)\) with minimum-jerk trajectories.

4

Voltage waveform

Map \(x_c(t)\) into electrode voltages \(v(t)\), then check continuity and slew-rate demand.

5

Ion motion

Simulate the ion response and compare \(x(t)\) against \(x_c(t)\).

6

Excitation metric

Measure residual amplitude, residual energy proxy, and residual-motion spectrum.

Transport path

Minimum-jerk interpolation uses a smooth endpoint-constrained path:

\[ s(u)=10u^3-15u^4+6u^5,\qquad u=t/T \]

This gives zero velocity and acceleration at endpoints, reducing impulsive forcing compared with linear interpolation.

Excitation metrics

Residual motion is the difference between the simulated ion trajectory and the moving well center:

\[ r(t)=x(t)-x_c(t) \] \[ E_{\mathrm{res}}(t)=\frac12 m\left(\dot r(t)^2+\omega^2r(t)^2\right) \]

Key result

The project shows that excitation behavior emerges clearly from trajectory smoothness alone. Linear interpolation strongly excites oscillatory motion, while minimum-jerk transport suppresses the resonance peak and reduces residual energy by orders of magnitude.

Why this matters

For trapped-ion systems, transport speed and motional excitation are coupled design constraints. This page makes that tradeoff visible in a compact reproducible model.

Glossary

TermMeaning

Minimum-jerkSmooth interpolation with zero endpoint velocity and acceleration.
Residual motionDifference between ion position and target path, \(r(t)=x(t)-x_c(t)\).
