Monoidal Categories & Product Systems over N^k

Seminar report · 2026-04-23
home · notebook · cosine-constraint-lab

Speaker’s claim

“One approach to identifying the C*-algebra of a product system over N^k is to build a representation of it with the aim of applying the Gauge-Invariant Uniqueness theorem. The main problem is how to build the representation. This is complicated by the commutativity of N^k — so many balanced tensor-product correspondences are isomorphic. I'll show how an abstract categorical result of Fowler-Sims can be used to surmount this obstacle.”

Joint work with: Valentin Deaconu, Menevse Eryuzlu Paulovicks, and S. Kaliszewski

Fowler-Sims result Abstract categorical theorem used here to construct representations.
Monoidal category Category equipped with a tensor product and unit object.

The obstacle

\(N^k\) is commutative, so many balanced tensor-product correspondences become isomorphic. That makes representation-building non-trivial, even when the general identification strategy via Gauge-Invariant Uniqueness is already known.
